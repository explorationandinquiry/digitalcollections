The basics


What are digital collections?

Digital collections are digital resources that feature archival and primary source materials. This can include historical newspapers, photographs, maps, drawings, manuscripts, rare books, posters, and audio recordings.

UCLA Library has a number of digital collections, including:
-UCLA Digital Collections
-IDEP
-Sinai

Why use digital collections?

Digital collections contain rare, ephemeral, and archival materials that can be hard to find elsewhere. Their holdings can help you learn about different communities and subjects, and can further your research.

UCLA Library Special Collections houses some of the physical originals for the digital collections at LINK. Browsing the collection online can give you a sense of if you'd like to request to see some of these materials in person.

The modules

This website features three modules for exploring UCLA's digital collections. Each module will teach you different skills for understanding how to use digital collections and evaluate their materials.

Strategic Search

This module will teach you how to leverage the interface of digital collections to find items of interest. Structured as a scavenger hunt, you'll use different search techniques to find items. Get started >>

Points of View

Primary sources are created by people, carrying with them the worldview, expertise, and biases of their creators. This module will teach you how to interrogate and evaluate primary sources. Get started >>

Presence and Absence

Like libraries, archives, and museums, digital collections contain records that document and preserve cultural histories. This module will ask you to think of whose voices are present, and whose are missing. Get started >>
